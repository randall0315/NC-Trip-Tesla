⚠ No Superchargers in the mountains — but you have two backstops

There is no Supercharger near Hayesville, and nothing fast in that whole corner. Leave Atlanta at 100% — from full you reach Hayesville (~100 mi) easily. The good news: you have two ways to keep topped up during the stay, so this isn't the dead zone it first looks like.

1 · The house outlet (primary). A standard wall outlet adds ~3–4 mi/hr — left plugged in day and night, that's ~60–80 mi/day and creeps up to ~80% over a few days. This works as long as daily driving stays modest. Heavy running-around days can out-pace it, so watch the trend rather than assuming it always recovers overnight. Checking on a 240V outlet at the house — if it's there, bring the Mobile Connector + adapter and the house becomes a real overnight charge.

2 · Harrah's Cherokee Valley River, Murphy (fast catch-up). ~25 min from Hayesville. 24 free Tesla Destination chargers, Level 2 (~40–50 mi/hr) — not a Supercharger, but park for dinner with Luca one evening and come back with 80–100+ mi added. This is your reset if the house outlet falls behind, and it solves the drive home.

For the drive home: top up (house + a Murphy visit if needed) so you leave with enough to reach the I-75 Superchargers (Calhoun / Buc-ee's) — possibly 60–90 mi out. Go with a comfortable buffer; a few of Murphy's ports are sometimes down, so don't roll in on fumes.

Wednesday Atlanta → Hayesville · via GA-515 · ~120 mi · ~2.5 hrs

The easy leg. Loop the west side of Atlanta on I-285, up I-575 onto GA-515 (the Georgia Mountain Parkway) — a four-lane divided highway through valley towns. No switchbacks, real shoulders, gas and food the whole way.

⚠ One nav trap: don't let it route you over Blood Mountain

Stay on the 515 corridor through Blue Ridge → Blairsville. If the Tesla or Google Maps ever tries to "shortcut" you onto US-19 or US-129 through Dahlonega / Cleveland, reject it — that's the steep two-lane switchback route over Blood Mountain. The 515 way is longer in miles but easier, calmer, and better with Luca in the car.

Before you go

Charge to 100% overnight

Both the night before departure and the night before leaving Atlanta. Free range while you sleep — no reason not to.

House outlet: keep it plugged in

A standard outlet nets ~60–80 mi/day if plugged in continuously and daily driving stays light. Checking on a 240V outlet at the house — bring the Mobile Connector + adapter in case it's there.

Download Luca's shows offline

Bluey, on the tablet, downloaded — not streaming. Mountain dead zones make a streaming-dependent tablet useless with a restless toddler.

Pack layers

Hayesville sits at elevation; June evenings run noticeably cooler than Tampa. Light jackets for all three of you.

Murphy casino = fast catch-up

Harrah's Cherokee Valley River, ~25 min away. 24 free Level 2 chargers (~40–50 mi/hr). Park for dinner one evening, gain 80–100+ mi. Your reset if the house outlet falls behind — and it covers the drive home.

Kimpton Sylvan — a couple of notes

Same Buckhead block as the route already assumes, so charging (Roswell Rd / Lenox) is unchanged. Two small heads-ups from reviews: coffee is lobby-only, no in-room maker — worth knowing before an early departure — and ask valet about EV charging if you want an overnight trickle, though Roswell Rd covers you regardless.

Dogs boarded

Confirm boarding drop-off and pack their food, meds, and bedding.

Optional stops — only if Luca needs one

The plan holds on its own, but the Lake Park → Macon stretch (~160 mi) is the long gap. These are the good bailout points along I-75 if you need a break between the planned stops.

Buc-ee's, Warner Robins Best family stop

~mile 335 · before Macon

The giant Buc-ee's — 24/7, famously clean private restrooms, endless food, easy on/off I-75. A genuine event for a kid. Has EV chargers but not Superchargers, so treat it as a break, then charge at Macon ~25 min later. If you make one bonus stop all day, make it this.

Florida Welcome Center, Jennings

~mile 200 · FL/GA line

The best pure leg-stretch on Day 1 — free orange juice, very clean restrooms, room to run around, right at the state line. No charging, just a clean easy break before the first Supercharger.

Tifton Supercharger

~mile 270 · splits the long gap

If you'd rather split the charge than do one long Macon stop. Starbucks and fast food walkable. Honest caveat: no public restroom here and slower chargers (~70kW) — more a coffee/food stop than a bathroom or fast-charge one.

Ashburn Supercharger (charging backup)

~mile 295

Fast — 15+ stalls, often empty, can hit 250kW, Waffle House & McDonald's walkable. The Circle K it sits at is grim, but if you need a quick fast-charge it does the job.

Mercier Orchards, Blue Ridge Day 2

on the GA-515 leg

The standout Day 2 break — huge family-friendly orchard, fresh bakery and cider, tractor ride, outdoor tables, dog-friendly, great views. A real stop, not a gas station. Apples, bathrooms, room to roam.
